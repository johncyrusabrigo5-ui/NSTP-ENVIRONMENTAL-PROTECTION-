I. What is Environmental Protection?

Environmental protection is the practice of protecting the natural environment for the benefit of both the environment and humans. According to our module, we must address the rapidly increasing cases of environmental degradation and inequality in our country to safeguard our future.

II. Contemporary Environmental Issues

1. Threatened Biodiversity: The Philippines ranks 4th in the Asia-Pacific for threatened species. We have 784 threatened animals (fauna) and 984 threatened plants (flora).

2. Mining and Overextraction: This leads to the destruction of forests and oceans, causing landslides, erosion hazards, and water pollution.

3. Coastal Degradation: As a "coral hot spot," it is alarming that 40% of corals in Philippine waters are already in bad condition.

III. Environmental Justice and Alternatives

Environmental justice incorporates two important concepts: Social Inclusion (taking care of the well-being of the poor) and Sustainability. Some innovative alternatives include:

  • Closed Loop System: An alternative that mimics natural cycles to focus on zero waste.
  • Local Living Economies: Supporting local enterprises that provide fair jobs and a healthy environment.
  • Collective Actions: Various community efforts to uphold environmental rights.

IV. Petition to the Government

"I urge our officials to prioritize the protection of our natural resources over destructive economic systems. We need governance that addresses inequality and ensures that our environmental abundance actually benefits the poor. We must demand accountability to stop the overextraction of minerals that destroys our biodiversity and harms our marginalized communities."

V. The Role of the Youth

The youth are not passive victims of environmental degradation. We can work in solidarity with poor communities, learn more about environmental issues, educate other people, and extend technical assistance to help preserve our natural wealth.
